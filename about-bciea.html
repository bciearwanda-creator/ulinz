Who We Are

A survivor-centered movement for breast health, dignity, and hope.

BCIEA works to reduce fear, silence, misinformation, and late action around breast cancer.

Our Mission

To increase breast cancer awareness, promote early detection, support survivors, and mobilise communities through education, compassion, and innovation.

What BCIEA Does

Programs that turn awareness into action.

These program areas connect directly to Ulinzi fundraising, sponsor support, volunteer work, and year-round community engagement.

Community Education

Breast health conversations, awareness workshops, village outreach, and myth-breaking education for families and communities.

Survivor Support

Safe spaces, survivor group meetings, emotional support, practical guidance, and dignity-centered care for women and families.

IROZA Innovation

A multilingual AI education companion designed to help people ask questions and learn about breast health in accessible language.

Wearables for Impact

Ulinzi and IKUNDE products that help raise funds and spread awareness through purpose-driven fashion.

Partnerships

Collaboration with health institutions, sponsors, volunteers, media, and community leaders to increase reach and trust.

Ulinzi Walk

The annual public movement that brings people together to register, donate, volunteer, sponsor, and walk for life.

Roadmap

BCIEA Roadmap to 2030

A future-facing plan for awareness, survivor support, digital education, and stronger national and diaspora participation.

1

2026 — Ulinzi Platform Launch

Register walkers, raise funds, sell wearables, recruit volunteers, attract sponsors, and collect stories.

2

2027 — Community Expansion

Grow outreach through more villages, schools, workplaces, and regional ambassadors.

3

2028 — IROZA Scale-Up

Expand digital breast health education through local languages and mobile-first access.

4

2030 — Stronger National Movement

Build a trusted, year-round movement that supports early action, survivor dignity, and community awareness.

Our Vision for 2030

A Rwanda where every person understands breast health, every family knows where to seek support, and every survivor receives dignity, care, and hope.
