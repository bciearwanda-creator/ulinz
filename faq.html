Event Questions

Ulinzi is BCIEA’s breast cancer awareness walk and year-round movement. It brings together survivors, families, students, companies, volunteers, healthcare workers, and supporters to raise awareness, fund survivor support, and expand education across Rwanda.
The prototype uses October 2026 at Kigali Golf Course as the event placeholder. The final date, location, and route can be updated once BCIEA confirms official details.
Yes. Ulinzi is designed for families, students, companies, survivors, and community groups. Registration packages include individual, student, family, corporate team, VIP, and survivor-sponsored ticket options.
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Pink or white clothing
  • Water bottle
  • Your QR ticket
  • Sun protection
  • A phone for photos and updates

Registration Questions

Go to register.html, choose your package, fill in your details, select shirt size, then complete payment. The current page is a front-end prototype and can later connect to a database and payment system.
Yes. Corporate teams, school groups, churches, families, and community groups can register together. The registration page includes a team option ready for future backend connection.
Yes, the intended system is to generate a QR ticket after successful registration and payment. The QR ticket can be used for event check-in, shirt pickup, and registration verification.

Donation Questions

Donations support survivor care, rural awareness campaigns, community outreach, village ambassadors, Pink Wellness activities, and IROZA education. Donation categories include sponsor a survivor, sponsor a village ambassador, memorial donation, and corporate donation.
Yes. The diaspora page is designed for supporters in the USA, Canada, UK, Belgium, Australia, Europe, and other countries to donate, sponsor survivors, register teams, or buy merchandise.
The future system should send email receipts automatically after successful donations. Donors should also be able to view donation history from their account dashboard later.

Volunteer Questions

Visit volunteer.html, choose a role, add your contact details, availability, and skills, then submit the form.
Roles include registration, social media, health education, logistics, photography, sponsorship support, vendor recruitment, and event-day support.
Yes. Volunteer training dates and assignments should be shown in the volunteer dashboard once the backend is added.

Sponsor Questions

Companies can visit sponsor.html, review sponsorship packages, request the sponsorship deck, and contact the BCIEA team through the sponsor inquiry form.
Yes, sponsor packages can include booth space, event village presence, logo placement, social media recognition, speaking opportunities, and community outreach sponsorship.

IROZA Questions

IROZA is BCIEA’s innovation and education tool designed to help people access breast health information in simple language, with a focus on awareness, early guidance, and community education.
The planned language support includes Kinyarwanda, English, and French, with optional Swahili later.
Yes. Partners can support IROZA through sponsorship, technical collaboration, community education, language support, or digital outreach campaigns.

Wall of Honor Questions

Go to wall-of-honor.html, add the name, photo, short tribute, and choose whether to dedicate a donation.
Yes. The Wall of Honor is designed to connect emotional tributes with survivor support and memorial donations.

Media Questions

Journalists can visit media.html for press releases, photos, videos, logos, fact sheets, hashtags, and media contact details.
Use the media inquiry form on the media page or the general contact page to request interviews, event access, press coverage, or official information.
